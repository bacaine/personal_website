is a computational chemist.

I have a first class MChem(Hons) degree and a PhD from the university of manchester. I completed my PhD in the development of an in silico pKa prediction tool, called AIBL-pKa, under the supervision of Professor Paul Popelier. My time in academia allowed me to develop skills and knowledge in many areas of computational and theoretical chemistry and cheminformatics, these include:

  • ab initio calculations and quantum chemical theory
  • QSAR for ADMET profiling
  • machine learning theory and some application using python libraries
  • scientific programming with bash/python/FORTRAN/MATLAB.

my interests lie in method development in the context of computational approaches to property prediction of small molecules. this includes both development of theory and the design and engineering of automated tools.
